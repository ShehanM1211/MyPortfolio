Project Discription

The project is a Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis of a wind turbine aerofoil at various angles of attack. The main focus of the study is to analyze the aerodynamic efficiency of the NACA 0012 airfoil under different conditions while maintaining a constant Reynolds number. The project utilizes Ansys-Fluent software to simulate the flow around the aerofoil. Key components of the project include:

  • Assumptions and Simplifications:- These include steady-state flow, incompressible flow, two-dimensional analysis, and ignoring gravity. The Realizable k-Îµ turbulence model is utilized due to its effectiveness in managing high angles of attack.
  • Flow Domain and Computational Mesh:- Details the geometry setup, chord length, outer circle diameter, and mesh specifics like the number of elements and nodes.
  • Simulation Settings:- Covers the settings used in Ansys-Fluent, including convective and diffusive flux calculation schemes and pressure-velocity coupling.
